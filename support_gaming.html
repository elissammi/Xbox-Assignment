Xbox Game Pass subscription

Game Pass Ultimate is required for cloud gaming.
See what else you can do to get the most out of your subscription.

View and manage your subscriptions on Xbox

To see your subscriptions and find the latest offers:

  1. Press the Xbox button on your controller to open the guide, and then select Profile & system > Settings > Account > Subscriptions.
  2. Select the subscription you want to manage.

From here, you can view your payment schedule, change your payment option, and check out features and benefits such as free games and weekly deals.

You can also view the status of your subscriptions and manage them online by signing in to your Services & subscriptions page with your Microsoft account.

Xbox subscription cancellation and refunds

To cancel and get a refund for your subscription (if eligible):

  1. Go to the Services & subscriptions page for your account.
  2. Select Manage next to the subscription you want to cancel.
  3. Select Cancel subscription or Turn off recurring billing
    • If you choose Cancel subscription and you’re eligible for a refund, you’ll be provided two options: Don’t charge me on (next renewal date) and Cancel immediately and get a refund.
    • If you choose Cancel subscription but you don’t qualify for a refund, you’ll be prompted to Turn off recurring billing and won’t see a refund option. Turning off recurring billing will prevent the next charge.

Troubleshooting

Troubleshoot problems with cloud gaming

Troubleshoot network latency (lag)
Most audio and video issues are caused by problems with the network connection. The troubleshooting steps below can help ensure an optimal connection with your network:

  • If you're on a Wi-Fi network, make sure you're on a 5 Ghz network, not 2.4 Ghz (you may see interference on a 2.4 GHz network). For more info, see “Using a 5 GHz Wi-Fi network” below.
  • If you’re trying to cast your device to another screen, this will add latency to the output which may result in increased lag or the audio that’s out of sync.
  • If you’re using an Android device, clear the app cache. Go to Settings > Apps, select the Xbox Game Pass app, then select Storage > Clear cache.
  • Try connecting your controller directly to your device using a USB cable.
Troubleshoot issues in the Xbox Game Pass mobile app

The app has crashed or closed unexpectedly
After you try each of the following troubleshooting steps, try opening the app again. If the app continues to crash or fails to open, try the next step.

  1. Close the app from the recent applications menu.
  2. Restart your phone.
  3. Clear the app cache. Go to Settings > Apps, select the app, then select Storage > Clear cache.
  4. Clear the app storage then try again. Go to Settings > Apps, select the app, then select Storage > Clear storage.
  5. Uninstall and reinstall. Press on the app icon until you get the option to Uninstall. Then reinstall the app from Play Store.

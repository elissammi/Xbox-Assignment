Manage privacy in Xbox apps

Control how you play

Xbox online safety and privacy settings let you decide who can see your Xbox profile details and how people can interact with you and your content.

Learn about Xbox privacy settings for online safety and privacy

Use these privacy settings to choose how others see your Xbox profile, who can communicate with you, and how they can interact with your content.

  1. Press the Xbox button to open the guide.
  2. Select Profile & system > Settings > Account > Privacy & online safety > Xbox privacy.
  3. Select the Child, Teen, or Adult default settings, or customise your settings by selecting View details & customise.
  4. Select Contact preferences to choose whether you’d like to receive offers from Xbox.

Manage a member's online safety and privacy settings

The Xbox Family Settings app provides a simple and convenient way for organisers in a family group set members’ social communication and multiplayer privacy settings from an iOS or Android mobile device.

How to manage your activity feed and privacy

The activity feed lets you create posts for others on the Xbox network to see and share your gaming activity. If you only want your friends to see what you’re up to, you can adjust your settings for more privacy.

Whose content can I see in activity feeds?
Adult accounts can see screenshots, game clips, and broadcasts made by anyone else on Xbox.
Options under Game content include:

  • You can share game clips and screenshots
  • Others can see your game clips and screenshots
  • You can see and upload community creations
  • You can broadcast gameplay
  • You can share content made using Kinect or another camera

Choose a designation from each option (such as Block) to narrow whose comments and content you can see or allow to see.
Default settings for child and teen accounts are more restrictive, but they can be adjusted by an organizer in the family group.

Support for a safe gaming experience

Keep kids safe on Xbox

Set an age limit for content on Xbox
When a member in your family group makes a request to use a blocked app or game that’s rated above their content restrictions, you can approve it as the organizer in your family group.
To grant content exceptions for members in your family group:

  1. Locate the content that you want to grant permission for a member to access in My games & apps.
  2. On the Choose who will give permission screen, select your account.
  3. If prompted, sign in to your account, or use the console’s passkey.
  4. Select either Always or Just this once (or only Just this once if your account is not on the console).
Microsoft account and Xbox data usage

Manage settings for optional data sharing
Xbox consoles collect and send data to Microsoft about how they’re used. This data includes:

  • Required service data for features, services, and experiences
  • Required service data for games and apps that you use on the console
  • Required diagnostic data
